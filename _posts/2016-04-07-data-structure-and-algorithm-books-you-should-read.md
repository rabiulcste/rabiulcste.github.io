***Top 5 Data Structure and Algorithm Books I would like to recommend:***

- <span class="qlink_container">[***Introduction to Algorithms by Thomas H. Cormen***](http://www.amazon.com/Introduction-Algorithms-Edition-Thomas-Cormen/dp/0262033844?tag=javamysqlanta-20)</span> – This is one of the best books on Computer Algorithms, it’s written by four authors, one of them is Thomas H. Cormen, whose another book Unlocked Algorithm is also the most recommended book to learn algorithms. This book is a lot more comprehensive and covers lots of different algorithm and advanced problem-solving technique e.g. greedy algorithms, dynamic programming, Amortized Analysis, along with elementary data structures like Stacks and Queues, Array and linked list, Hash tables, Tree, and Graph.


- <span class="qlink_container">[***The Algorithm Design Manual by Skiena*** ](http://www.amazon.com/Algorithm-Design-Manual-Steven-Skiena/dp/1849967202)</span>– Lots of algorithmic problems, and discussions, war stories, related problems, interesting exercises. It helps in modeling a problem in different ways. A must work book for every passionate programmer. Don’t read this unless you have good insight into algorithms.
- <span class="qlink_container">[***Algorithms (4th Edition) by Robert Sedgewick , Kevin Wayne***](http://www.amazon.com/Algorithms-4th-Edition-Robert-Sedgewick/dp/032157351X)</span> – Relatively beginner level book, covers graphs, strings, hashing, searching, sorting, etc. very well. It follows OOP approach in Java. Strongly recommended for beginners, though nothing stops a professional. Web portal containing plenty of interesting exercises. There are other books by Sedgewick on Algorithms. Recommended for data structure learning.
- <span class="qlink_container">[***The Art of Computer Programming, 1st Edition by Donald E. Knuth***](http://www.amazon.com/Computer-Programming-Volumes-1-4A-Boxed/dp/0321751043)</span> – The Art of Computer Programming provide comprehensive overview of a wide variety of algorithms and the analysis. The first book begins with mathematical preliminaries, stacks, queues, lists, arrays, and trees–and presents implementations. Volume 2 of Donald Knuth’s classic series The Art of Computer Programming covers semi-numerical algorithms. Third volume is the most comprehensive survey of classical computer techniques for sorting and searching.
- <span class="qlink_container">[**Algorithm Design (English) 1st Edition**](http://www.flipkart.com/algorithm-design-english-1st/p/itmdx9sgrmh4g7af?pid=9789332518643&icmpid=reco_pp_hsame_book_book_5&ppid=9788131703106)</span> – An excellent book which is having a good flow and can be read with the easiness of reading a story. Excellent connections between chapters and all the concepts explained in a simple and layman way. I have read part of CLRS but got bored in between, but having this book made me a lot better in understanding the concepts of algorithms.  
  NB: Reading CLRS after reading this book will be recommended, so as to thorough the concepts.
